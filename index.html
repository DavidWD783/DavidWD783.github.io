What you need to know about the spring classics in cycling...

The spring classics in cycling begin in February with Omloop Het Nieuwsblad and last until April, ending with "La Doyenne", "the old lady", or Liege-Bastogne-Liege. The races are extremely long and often cover both the flat farmland and rolling "bergs" of France, Holland, and primarily Belgium. The racers that typically win the spring classics are considered some of the strongest bike racers in the world (see: Peter Sagan, John Degenkolb, Fabian Cancellara, Tom Boonen). With nick-names like Spartacus, Hercules, Tornado Tom, and Breakaway-Killer it's no wonder these men have dominated the races. Below, you can find a listing of each race in the classics calendar and some details of the race with images, video, and previous winners.

The spring classics in cycling cover the following races:

  • Ronde van Vlaanderen(De Ronde) - Belgium, April 3
  • Paris-Roubaix("Queen of the Classics" and "Hell of the North") - France, April 10
